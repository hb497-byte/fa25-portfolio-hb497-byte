In this project, I designed a mechanical lifting system within a defined 2D workspace measuring 150 cm in length and 50 cm in height. The goal was to maximize both the lifted weight and the achievable height of lift using only rigid components: a fixed-length bar, three pin supports (two of which were grounded), and a linear actuator selected from an online catalog based on its maximum force rating.

The design process involved applying principles of statics and mechanics of solids to determine optimal geometric arrangements and loading conditions. By analyzing equilibrium, moment arms, and force transmission through the structure, I developed a frame mechanism capable of efficiently converting the actuator’s linear motion into vertical lifting motion.

This assignment emphasized the integration of analytical reasoning with creative mechanical design—requiring consideration of both performance and physical constraints. The final submission included a detailed hand sketch illustrating the configuration, support placement, and actuator orientation.
